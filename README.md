## Research notes behind this repository

This README is based on public ad platform guidance, FTC endorsement guidance and practical AI video creative workflows.

### TikTok creative patterns

TikTok for Business recommends TikTok-first creative, vertical 9:16 format, safe zone awareness, sound or music, creator-style videos and regular creative testing. TikTok also emphasizes a structure that can be simplified as:

```text
hook -> body -> close
```

Useful references:

- https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article?aid=11953
- https://ads.tiktok.com/business/en/blog/creative-best-practices-top-performing-ads
- https://ads.tiktok.com/business/creative-center

### Meta Reels and creator-style ad patterns

Meta video and Reels guidance emphasizes mobile-first formats, vertical creative, safe zones, audio quality, creative variation and ad asset optimization. UGC-style videos often work because they feel native, but they still need brand safety, claim accuracy and platform-ready framing.

Useful references:

- https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/video-ad-format
- https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/facebook-instagram-reels-ads
- https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/meta-advantage-plus/creative

### Google and YouTube creative guidance

Google Ads creative guidance uses Google AI to detect missing video creative best practices and recommends that advertisers optimize video creative based on measurable attributes. YouTube and Google Ads support several video formats, including Shorts, skippable in-stream, non-skippable in-stream, in-feed video and bumper ads.

Useful references:

- https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/13812351
- https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/2375464

### FTC endorsement and disclosure guidance

The FTC says endorsements must be truthful and not misleading. If there is a material connection between an endorser and a brand, that connection should be clearly disclosed. FTC guidance also says endorsers should not talk about their experience with a product they have not tried and should not make claims that require proof the advertiser does not have.

Useful references:

- https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/media-resources/truth-advertising/advertisement-endorsements
- https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/ftcs-endorsement-guides

## Core principle: UGC style is not permission to fake real experience

UGC-style video can mean:

- handheld camera style
- natural creator delivery
- casual home setting
- direct-to-camera hook
- product demonstration
- relatable problem
- quick proof moment
- native social pacing

It should not mean:

- fake customer testimony
- fake before/after result
- fake expert endorsement
- fake medical claim
- fake earnings claim
- fake app review
- fake product experience
- undisclosed paid endorsement
- impersonation

A safe UGC-style prompt should describe **a creator-style ad concept**, not a fake real customer experience.

## AI UGC video prompt formula

Use this structure for most UGC-style videos:

```text
Create a [duration]-second vertical UGC-style video for [product/service].
Audience: [specific audience].
Creator role: [founder / brand creator / product demonstrator / educator / customer-style actor].
Disclosure context if needed: [sponsored/ad/brand demo].
Hook: [first 1 to 3 seconds].
Problem or desire: [what the viewer cares about].
Product moment: [show the product or app clearly].
Demo or proof moment: [visual demonstration, not unsupported claim].
Camera: [handheld but smooth, close-up, phone-style, creator framing].
Setting: [kitchen, bathroom, desk, car, gym, office, store, etc.].
Audio if supported: [natural voice, room tone, music].
CTA: [short and natural].
Restrictions: [no fake claims, no fake testimonials, no fake expert, no distorted hands, no face warping, no label distortion].
```

Example:

```text
Create a 9-second vertical UGC-style video for a meal planning app.
Audience: busy parents who do not know what to cook after work.
Creator role: brand creator demonstrating the app.
Disclosure context if needed: ad-style product demo.
Hook: creator opens the fridge and says, "I had chicken, rice and no idea what to cook."
Problem: decision fatigue at dinner time.
Product moment: phone screen shows abstract recipe cards without readable fake text.
Demo moment: creator taps one card and a finished dinner appears on the table.
Camera: handheld but smooth, natural kitchen close-up.
Setting: real home kitchen with daylight.
Audio if supported: natural voice, kitchen ambience, light upbeat music.
CTA: creator says, "Try it before dinner tonight."
Restrictions: no fake health claims, no fake testimonial, no unreadable UI text, no distorted hands, no extra people.
```

## UGC ad structure

A strong UGC-style ad usually follows this:

```text
1. Hook
2. Relatable problem
3. Product appears
4. Simple demo
5. Believable outcome
6. Natural CTA
```

### Hook

The first seconds answer:

```text
Why should I stop scrolling?
```

### Relatable problem

The viewer should recognize themselves.

### Product appears

Show the product early enough.

### Simple demo

One clear product action beats five vague benefits.

### Believable outcome

Show a plausible result, not an exaggerated guarantee.

### Natural CTA

Avoid corporate language. Make the ending feel native.

## UGC video types

### Founder-style UGC

Best when the founder can credibly explain the product.

Prompt angle:

```text
Founder explains why the product was built and shows one useful feature.
```

### Product demonstrator UGC

Best for physical products, apps and tools.

Prompt angle:

```text
Creator shows the product in use without making personal testimonial claims.
```

### Problem-solution UGC

Best for direct response.

Prompt angle:

```text
Start with a relatable problem, then show product solving one narrow part of it.
```

### Educational UGC

Best for SaaS, AI tools, courses, apps and B2B products.

Prompt angle:

```text
Creator teaches one useful thing and introduces the product as the workflow.
```

### Review-style UGC

Use carefully.

Prompt angle:

```text
Use as a fictional ad concept or product demo, not a fake real customer review.
```

### App demo UGC

Best for mobile apps and SaaS tools.

Prompt angle:

```text
Creator shows phone or dashboard screen with abstract UI, no fake readable text.
```

## Platform-first UGC planning

### TikTok

Recommended:

- vertical 9:16
- hook in first seconds
- natural creator delivery
- product appears early
- native phone camera feel
- clear sound or captions
- safe-zone centered subject
- regular creative refresh

Prompt line:

```text
Style: TikTok-native UGC ad, handheld but smooth, fast first-second hook, product shown early, natural creator tone.
```

### Instagram Reels

Recommended:

- vertical 9:16
- clean creator framing
- polished but not overproduced
- product visible early
- cover frame in mind
- CTA-safe final frame

Prompt line:

```text
Style: Instagram Reels UGC ad, vertical mobile-first, clean creator framing, product and CTA inside safe zones.
```

### Facebook Feed and Reels

Recommended:

- clear problem-solution
- readable on small screens
- product clarity
- safe claims
- square and vertical variants

Prompt line:

```text
Style: mobile-first Facebook UGC ad with clear problem-solution structure and product-centered final frame.
```

### YouTube Shorts

Recommended:

- strong first frame
- clear creator hook
- product demo quickly
- thumbnail-like final beat
- vertical 9:16

Prompt line:

```text
Style: YouTube Shorts UGC ad with strong visual hook, quick demo and final frame suitable for a thumbnail.
```

### YouTube in-stream

Recommended:

- product appears before skip moment
- more structured explanation
- natural creator or founder voice
- 15 to 30 seconds if needed

Prompt line:

```text
Style: creator-led YouTube in-stream ad opening with problem, product, demo and CTA before the viewer loses attention.
```

## Workflow: compliant testimonial-style structure

Use “testimonial-style” only as a storytelling structure unless you have a real testimonial.

Safe structure:

```text
Creator demonstrates a product and describes the visible workflow.
```

Risky structure:

```text
Fake customer says the product changed their life.
```

Safer prompt line:

```text
Creator demonstrates the product as an ad concept. Do not present the creator as a real customer and do not include fake personal results.
```

Avoid:

- "I lost 20 pounds"
- "I made $10,000"
- "This cured my skin"
- "I guarantee this works"
- "My doctor recommended it" unless true and documented
- fake review stars
- fake app store ratings
- fake before/after claims

## Common mistakes

### Mistake 1: Confusing UGC style with fake UGC

UGC style can be simulated as a creative format. Fake real customer testimony is risky and misleading.

### Mistake 2: Hiding the product too long

Show the product early enough for viewers to understand relevance.

### Mistake 3: Making impossible claims

If the product claim needs proof, do not invent it.

### Mistake 4: Overproducing

UGC should feel natural. Too much polish can make it feel like a corporate ad.

### Mistake 5: Forgetting disclosure

If there is a material connection, disclosure should be clear and hard to miss.

### Mistake 6: Trusting fake UI text

Add important UI text later or keep generated UI abstract.

### Mistake 7: No landing page match

Do not show features, offers or claims that are not supported by the landing page.

## FAQ

### What is an AI UGC video prompt?

An AI UGC video prompt is a structured instruction for generating a creator-style video ad with a hook, product moment, simple demo, natural setting, CTA and safety restrictions.

### What is UGC-style video?

UGC-style video is content that feels native to social platforms: handheld camera, natural delivery, direct-to-camera hook, product demo and casual setting.

### Is AI UGC the same as fake testimonial content?

No. UGC style is a creative format. Fake testimonials, fake reviews and fake customer experiences should be avoided.

### Can I generate testimonial-style videos?

You can use the structure of a testimonial-style ad only if you avoid fake claims and fake personal experience. Use real testimonials only when they are genuine and properly disclosed.

### What should a UGC video prompt include?

Include audience, creator role, hook, problem, product moment, demo, camera style, setting, audio, CTA and restrictions.

### What is the best format for UGC ads?

For TikTok, Reels and Shorts, vertical 9:16 is usually the priority.

### Should the product appear early?

Yes. Product relevance should usually be clear in the first few seconds.

### Can I use AI-generated UGC commercially?

Commercial use depends on platform terms, model terms, input rights, output rights, likeness rights, advertising rules and disclosure requirements.

### What should I avoid?

Avoid fake testimonials, fake reviews, fake expert claims, medical or financial claims without proof, fake UI, fake app ratings, celebrity impersonation and unsupported results.

### Do I need disclosures?

If there is a material connection between a brand and endorser, FTC guidance says that connection should be clearly disclosed. Other jurisdictions may have their own rules.
